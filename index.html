Is it the same for us when we happen upon near-revelation about our own self? When we see something of ourselves so perfectly reflected in the world, it’s off-putting. It’s a tear in your own sense of unique identity, for one. Like when a stranger puts you in a stereotyped box—and it hits so hard because you realize that what they said is not untrue. You’re not unique. You’re just another replaceable wheel in the greater machine. The grand story you’ve been telling yourself is wrong.

Spend enough time living in a big city and you'll eventually see your friends walking around in bodies that don’t belong to them. You see them rounding a corner, and you excitedly think of ways to pounce on them from behind, but then something’s off. You squint, move closer, teetering between recognition and caution. It’s not them. But it looks so close that you almost want to take a picture of this freak of nature, just to show people back home.
